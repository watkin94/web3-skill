# Participation And Crypto Culture

## Scope

Use this file when the question sounds like "币圈入门":

- how to start participating
- what people mean by spot, leverage, narrative, meme, airdrop, farming
- what exchanges and on-chain apps each do
- how people move between fiat, exchanges, wallets, and on-chain protocols

## The Usual Newcomer Path

1. Learn core terms and risks.
2. Buy or receive a small amount of a major asset or stablecoin.
3. Withdraw to a self-custody wallet only after understanding addresses and networks.
4. Try one simple on-chain action:
   - swap
   - bridge
   - stake
   - use a lending protocol
5. Keep records and review permissions after each step.

## Centralized Exchange Vs On-Chain

- `Centralized exchange (CEX)`: easier onboarding, fiat rails, account-based, custodied by the exchange.
- `On-chain / DEX`: self-custody, transparent settlement, usually requires a wallet and Gas.

Simple rule:
- Use CEX for onboarding and off-ramping.
- Use on-chain apps when you understand wallet safety, network selection, and transaction review.

## Topics To Explain Clearly

### Spot

Buying and holding an asset directly. Lower complexity than derivatives but still volatile.

### Perpetuals / Futures

Leveraged derivative products. High liquidation risk. Not suitable as a default beginner recommendation.

### Stablecoins

Useful as a bridge between fiat thinking and on-chain usage, but still carry issuer, depeg, or bridge risk.

### DeFi

Includes swapping, liquidity provision, lending, borrowing, staking, and yield strategies.

### Airdrops

Tokens distributed to users under certain rules. Explain that many "claim" links are scams and that past behavior does not guarantee future rewards.

### Meme Coins

Community-driven assets with extreme volatility and reflexive attention dynamics. Explain narrative risk and liquidity risk before anything else.

### NFT

Digital assets with unique IDs. Useful for art, identity, ticketing, and access, but the market can be very speculative.

## Market And Community Concepts

- `Narrative`: the story that drives attention, such as AI, modularity, restaking, memes, or gaming.
- `Liquidity`: how easily an asset can be bought or sold without moving the price.
- `Volatility`: how sharply the price moves.
- `Cycle`: broad market phase such as bull, bear, accumulation, or euphoria.
- `Rotation`: capital moving from one theme to another.

## Trading Slang And How To Explain It

When users ask about slang, answer the way people in the market actually use the term, then translate it into plain language.

Good pattern:

- `土狗`: "就是高风险小币，可能飞，也可能直接归零。"
- `接盘`: "就是别人想卖的时候，你去高位接了。"
- `埋人`: "就是让后来的人套在里面，很难体面出来。"

Keep the explanation short. The goal is recognition, not a lecture.

Useful clusters:

- `price action`: 拉盘, 砸盘, 洗盘, 横盘, 插针, 瀑布
- `risk`: 归零, rug, 埋人, 接盘, 爆仓
- `market roles`: 庄, 主力, 聪明钱, 巨鲸, 韭菜
- `meme culture`: 土狗, 龙头, 打狗, 上车, 下车

## When A User Asks "How Do I Start?"

Prefer this order:

1. Learn wallet safety.
2. Use only major chains and major apps at first.
3. Start with tiny amounts.
4. Avoid leverage, blind following, and random tokens.
5. Keep a checklist for networks, contract approvals, and official links.

## Risk Framing

Do not give direct buy/sell calls.
If the user asks for investment opinions, shift to:

- what the asset is
- what problem it claims to solve
- token unlock and liquidity concerns
- why price can detach from fundamentals
- what risks a beginner often ignores

If the user is clearly just asking what a slang term means, do not turn it into investment advice. Just explain the term and the implied risk.
